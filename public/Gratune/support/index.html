Support for Gratune

Gratune is a local-first Christian gratitude app. If something is unclear or not working as expected, this page covers the basics and gives you a direct contact path.

Email: chaoliu@cedarville.edu

What Gratune does

Gratune guides you through a short daily practice with scripture, gratitude reflection, optional prayer, and private on-device journaling.

  • No account is required.
  • Your entries and settings stay on your device.
  • Reminders are optional and fully user-controlled.

Troubleshooting checklist

  • Update to the latest available version of Gratune in the App Store.
  • Restart the app if a screen appears stuck or a prompt does not respond.
  • Restart your iPhone if reminders, microphone access, or camera access behave unexpectedly.
  • Confirm permissions in iPhone Settings if voice, speech recognition, camera, or notifications do not work.

Before you email

Include enough detail to reproduce the issue quickly.

  • Your iPhone model and iOS version.
  • The Gratune app version.
  • What you expected to happen.
  • What happened instead.

Privacy questions

Gratune is designed as a private, local-first app. For privacy-specific questions, review the privacy page or email directly.

Frequently asked questions

Common support topics

Do I need an account?

No. Gratune does not require sign-in or account creation to use the app.

Where are my entries stored?

Your gratitude entries, prayers, and settings are stored on your device.

Why are reminders not appearing?

Check that notifications are enabled for Gratune in iPhone Settings and verify your selected reminder time inside the app.

Why does voice typing not work?

Confirm microphone and speech recognition permissions are enabled for Gratune in iPhone Settings.

Can I attach photos?

Yes. Gratune supports taking a photo for a gratitude entry when camera access is granted.

Does Gratune upload my journal?

No cloud journal sync is required for the current version of the app.
